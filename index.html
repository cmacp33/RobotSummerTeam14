Project Overview

This project was made in ENPH253, more commonly referred to as robot summer. ENPH253 is the one and only course taken by second year UBC Engineering Physics student in summer term 2. The class is divided into teams of 3-4 people and each team has 6 weeks to make a robot to compete in a unique challenge each year. Most teams, including ours, spent 60-70 hours a week in the lab attempting to make the best robot in the class. While the project took a lot of effort we all learned a lot about prototyping, troubleshooting, design, and much more.


Project Details

This year the competition was based on the video game Overcooked. Overcooked is a cooking game where you and 1-3 other chefs must work together to fulfill customer orders within a time limit. The game is pretty hard on its own but becomes much more of a challenge when you have to build robots to play it in real life. Our challenge was to be able to build two robots that could work together to make and assortment of burgers, fries, and salads (note: the 'food' was plastic) in under two minutes.
